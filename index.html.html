KS2 → KS3 Transition

What students bring from KS2 Foundation

Students arrive in Year 7 with procedural fluency in arithmetic, basic shape knowledge, and introductory algebra. However, depth varies enormously, many students have gaps in fractions, place value with decimals, and reasoning under exam conditions.

Key bridging topics Watch these

→
Place value & decimals, KS2 introduces 3 decimal places; KS3 extends to standard form, rounding to significant figures, and negative numbers on a number line.
→
Fractions, KS2 covers add/subtract and simple multiply; KS3 demands division of fractions and multiplication of mixed numbers. This is where many students hit a wall.
→
Algebra, KS2 introduces simple formulae and one-step equations; KS3 moves to unknowns on both sides, forming equations from context, and rearranging.
→
Four operations, KS2 uses written methods; KS3 applies all operations to negative numbers, fractions, and algebraic expressions.
→
Statistics, KS2 reads and interprets charts; KS3 requires choosing appropriate representations and calculating mean, median, mode, range.
→
Measurement, KS2 converts metric/imperial; KS3 extends to compound measures (speed, density) and scale drawings.

What this means for teaching Pedagogy

When a Year 7 student struggles with a KS3 topic, the gap is almost always in KS2. Diagnosing which KS2 concept is missing, rather than re-teaching the KS3 method, is what effective secondary maths teaching looks like.
